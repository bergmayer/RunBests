DESCRIPTION (4000 char max)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Apple Fitness is a fantastic tool, but it's missing one crucial feature: a way
to actually see your personal best running times.

That's exactly why Run Bests exists.

Runners rarely hit the exact same distance twice. Your "5-mile run" might be
5.1 miles today and 5.05 miles tomorrow. Because of that variation, tracking
your true PRs has always been a headache. Run Bests fixes that with smart
distance bracketing that uncovers the personal bests hidden in your workout
history.

Just set a target distance (in miles or kilometers) and choose a "margin of
error." For example, if you want to find your fastest 5-mile run, set a 0.25
margin and Run Bests will instantly scan your selected date range and pull
every run between 5.0 and 5.25 miles into one clean, sortable list.

KEY FEATURES

🏃 Smart Distance Bracketing
Group your past runs using a custom margin of error (0, 0.25, 0.5, or 1) so no
near-miss gets left out. Margin is applied upward only — a 4.99-mile workout
won't count toward 5-mile until it crosses the line.

⏱ Race Time vs. Workout Time
For each qualifying workout, see both the time you crossed the exact target
distance (race time, GPS-derived) and the full elapsed workout time. Each
metric has its own pace. Sort the top 20 by any of the four.

⚡ Best Segment Paces
Find your fastest contiguous 1 km, 1 mile (or any distance you pick) found
anywhere inside any run — even if your fastest mile happened in the middle of
a long marathon.

🏅 Major Races + Overall Records
Toggle Half Marathon and Marathon (21.0975 km and 42.195 km). Plus longest
distance, longest duration, and fastest average pace — all opt-in, all
configurable.

📅 Custom Date Filters
Browse PRs across all time, the last 30 / 90 days, the last year, a single
year, or any custom date range. Watch yourself improve across a training
cycle.

📊 Tap-to-Sort Leaderboards
Inside each category, top 20 by Race time, Race pace, Workout time, or
Workout pace. Tap any row to drill into the workout — route map, heart rate,
energy, temperature, the works.

🔒 Privacy by Design
Run Bests reads your running workouts from Apple Health and stores a private
local cache on your device. Nothing leaves your phone. No account, no
servers, no tracking.

WHY RUN BESTS

Most fitness apps either dump every workout into an endless feed or compute
arbitrary "PR" badges that ignore how messy real-world distances are. Run
Bests treats runners like adults: you tell it the distances you care about,
the margin you'll accept, and the time window — and it does the math.

================================================================================
PRESS / BLOG INTRO
================================================================================

A new iOS app called Run Bests aims to fill a long-standing gap in Apple's
fitness ecosystem: an actual personal-record leaderboard for runners.

While Apple Fitness diligently records every running workout, it offers no
native way to see your fastest time at any particular distance — and
distances in real-world running rarely line up exactly. A "5-mile run" might
clock in at 5.1 miles one day and 5.05 the next, leaving traditional PR
tracking either painfully manual or wildly imprecise.

Run Bests solves the problem with what its developer calls "smart distance
bracketing." Users set a target distance (in miles or kilometers) and a
margin of error — typically 0.25 mi — and the app instantly pulls every
qualifying run into a single sortable list. Each result shows both the time
it took to cover the exact target distance (computed from GPS) and the
full elapsed workout time, along with their respective paces.

The app is local-only, reads from Apple Health, and is free on the App
Store. [App Store link]
